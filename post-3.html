Before you drive away, know what your rental insurance covers and how to reduce risk:

What's typically included

  • Third‑party liability: covers damage or injury to others.
  • Collision damage: may cover vehicle damage but often with an excess.
  • Theft protection: covers vehicle theft, subject to conditions.

Excess & waivers

Most policies include an excess (the amount you're liable for after a claim). Consider a Collision Damage Waiver (CDW) or Super CDW to lower or remove excess — check daily cost and exclusions.

Optional extras to consider

  • Roadside assistance: helpful for breakdowns, tyre changes and towing.
  • Additional driver: add nominated drivers to ensure cover.
  • Personal effects protection: for items left in the vehicle.

Making a claim — quick steps

  1. Stop safely and contact the rental provider immediately.
  2. Take photos of damage, scene and other vehicles involved.
  3. Obtain a police report if required (the provider will advise).
  4. Keep receipts for any emergency costs and follow the provider's claims process.

Tip: read the rental agreement for exclusions (off‑road use, unlicensed drivers, alcohol limits) and keep documents and contact numbers handy.

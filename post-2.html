Choosing the right SUV

By Grace N. • 15 Feb 2025 • Guide
Home  •  Blog

Choosing an SUV is about matching the vehicle to your needs. Below are the practical factors to consider:

1. Size & passengers

Compact SUVs are great for city driving and tight parking. Midsize models add comfort and luggage space for families. Full-size SUVs suit larger groups or when you need towing capacity.

2. Luggage & load

Check boot volume with seats up and folded. If you carry sports kit, camping gear or multiple suitcases, prioritize cargo space over extra passenger seats.

3. Fuel economy & running costs

Diesel often gives better long-distance economy; petrol and hybrids can be cheaper for shorter trips. Compare typical consumption (km/l) and local fuel prices.

4. Transmission & terrain

Automatic transmissions are easier in traffic; manual may be preferred for control and slightly better economy. For rough roads, consider 4x4 or all-wheel-drive models with higher ground clearance.

5. Safety & comfort features

Look for ABS, multiple airbags, stability control, ISOFIX child anchors, and driver assists like reverse camera and parking sensors. Climate control and adjustable seats make longer journeys easier.

6. Insurance & extras

Confirm what the standard insurance covers and whether you need to add collision damage waiver (CDW), roadside assistance or extra driver options.

Quick selection guide

  • Solo / couple / city use: Compact SUV (economical, easy to park)
  • Family / weekend trips: Midsize SUV (more comfort & boot space)
  • Off-road / heavy load: Full-size / 4x4 (higher clearance, towing)

Tip: test-drive the shortlisted model with all passengers and luggage to ensure comfort and visibility before you book.

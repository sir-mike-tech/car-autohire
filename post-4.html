Efficient City Driving Tips to Save Fuel

City driving can be hard on fuel and vehicles. With a few practical habits you can reduce consumption, cut operating costs and make your urban journeys smoother — whether you're renting for a day or managing a corporate fleet.

Smooth Acceleration & Braking

Avoid aggressive starts and sudden braking. Gradual acceleration and anticipating traffic flow reduces fuel burn and wear on brakes.

Maintain Consistent Speed

Use the highest safe gear and maintain steady speeds where possible. Stop-start traffic drains fuel — consider alternative routes during peak hours.

Tyre Pressure & Condition

Under‑inflated tyres increase rolling resistance. Check tyre pressure before long city shifts and inspect for uneven wear.

Lighten the Load

Remove unnecessary items from the trunk and roof racks. Extra weight significantly affects fuel efficiency, especially in stop‑and‑go traffic.

Switch Off When Idle

If you expect to be stopped for more than 30 seconds, turn the engine off. Modern engines are more efficient than idling for extended periods.

Plan & Combine Trips

Group errands to avoid repeated short trips. Cold engines use more fuel; combining trips keeps the engine warm and efficient.

Quick vehicle checks

Before you set off, a short walkaround saves time later: check engine oil and coolant levels, ensure lights work, confirm tyre pressure and verify the spare and jack are present. Report any faults to our team immediately.
